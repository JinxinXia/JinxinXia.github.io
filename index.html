Jinxin Xia is a mathematical statistics master student in the Mathematics and Statistics Department at Wake Forest University. After receiving his bachelor's degree in statistics, finance and public administration from dual degree program running by Sichuan University and Colordao State University in 2018, he was a Truman Fellow at Sandia National Laboratories in Livermore, CA. He received his BS in math and computer science at Wake Forest in 2006 and his MA in math at Wake Forest in 2008.

His research interests include statistical genetics, numerical linear algebra, high performance computing, and optimization, particularly in developing algorithmic ideas that translate to improved implementations and more efficient software. His work has been recognized with the SIAM Linear Algebra Prize; three conference best paper awards, at SPAA, IPDPS, and ICDM; the C.V. Ramamoorthy Distinguished Research Award at UC Berkeley; and the ACM Doctoral Dissertation Award - Honorable Mention.
